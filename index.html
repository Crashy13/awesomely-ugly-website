WHAT IS THE DEFAULT STATE OF BURGER?




Imagine this, you move into a new neighborhood and are invited to a neighborhood cook out. It's the first time you'll be meeting most of your new neighbors and you're excited. So you get party ready and head down the street to the cook out. When you arrive, you're taken back to the backyard where the pool and grill are. You make yourself a little cocktail, nothing too crazy, don't want everyone thinking you're an alcoholic. The host of the cook out and current grill master comes and asks if you want a burger. You reply yes, because it's a cook out, and that's what they have. He says you got it and heads back to the grill. He brings you over a plate, what are you expecting to be on it? Is it just a patty on a bun? Is there cheese on it? Does it come with condiments and other accoutrements on it or do you dress it yourself? This is what leads to the age old question "What is the default state of a burger?"

The debate rages on. Which side of history will you be on?

IS THERE A SOLID ANSWER TO THE QUESTION?

This may be a question you have never contemplated, but now you will. And as you begin to research and ask around, you may find that it is a very contentious subject amongst people. If you look up the definition of 'burger' in the dictionary, you will find that it redirects to 'hamburger'. While this points to just a patty and a bun, there are those that will say while that may be the definition term, in people's minds, the burger will always come with cheese unless you specifically request that it not have cheese. They state 'burger' is different than saying 'hamburger' or 'cheeseburger'. It's two separate camps with passionate voices about what the true answer is. Now this may seem like a question that will never have a definitive answer, after all, who would decide which side is right?

WELL WHAT DO I DO NOW?

All that is left to do is bring this question into your friend circles and families, sit back, and watch the fireworks. In the end, it doesn't really matter what the default of burger is, but it's a lot of fun to talk about. Personally, if I was in the scenario from above, I would expect a hunk of meat in a bun. Any good respectable cook out will have a table with different toppings for your burger, something like this:

  • Lettuce
  • Tomato
  • Onion
  • Pickles
  • Jalapenos
  • Mustard
  • Ketchup
  • Mayonnaise
And if you are the grill master that is hosting the cook out, just knock out the unknown and when you ask someone if they want a burger, also ask if they want cheese or not on it.

All opinions expressed in the above are just that, opinions.
